HACKATHON

SVECW HACKATHON by DEPT of IT, DEC 2, 2022

Participating in a hackathon can be an exciting and challenging experience, and when you are the only junior among seniors, it can be even more daunting. However, it can also be a valuable opportunity to learn, grow, and showcase your skills. Being the only junior in a hackathon can feel intimidating at first, but it is important to remember that everyone is there to collaborate and work towards a common goal. You may feel like you have less experience than the other participants, but that does not mean you have less to offer. In fact, your unique perspective and fresh ideas can be a real asset to your team. To make the most of your participation in the hackathon, it is important to be proactive and communicate clearly with your team. This means speaking up and sharing your ideas, asking questions, and seeking feedback. It is also important to listen to the ideas and perspectives of your team members and work collaboratively towards a solution. Another way to make the most of the experience is to be open to learning from the other participants. Senior participants may have more experience or knowledge in certain areas, and you can learn from them by asking questions, observing their approach to problem-solving, and seeking their advice. It is also important to remember to take care of yourself during the hackathon. This means taking breaks when you need to, staying hydrated, and getting enough rest. It can be tempting to work around the clock to meet a deadline, but it is important to prioritize your well-being. At the end of the hackathon, win or lose, you will have gained valuable experience, learned from others, and demonstrated your skills and abilities. You may also have formed new connections and friendships with your fellow participants, which can be valuable resources for future projects and collaborations. In conclusion, participating in a hackathon with seniors as the only junior can be a challenging but rewarding experience. By being proactive, communicating clearly, and being open to learning from others, you can make the most of the opportunity and demonstrate your skills and abilities

DEBATE COMPETITION

ON VIOLENCE AGAINST WOMEN , DEC 2, 2022

Winning a debate competition on violence against women can be a powerful experience. Not only does it showcase your ability to research, construct arguments, and articulate your position, but it also allows you to be a voice for those who may not have one. The issue of violence against women is a critical one, and it requires a nuanced and empathetic approach to address. To win a debate competition on violence against women, it is essential to have a deep understanding of the issue. This means researching the prevalence and causes of violence against women, as well as the impact it has on individuals and society as a whole. It is also important to understand the various forms of violence that women experience, including physical, sexual, and emotional abuse. Once you have a solid understanding of the issue, it is time to construct your arguments. One effective way to approach this is to use statistics and evidence to support your position. For example, you could highlight the fact that 1 in 3 women globally have experienced physical or sexual violence in their lifetime, or that women who experience domestic violence are at a higher risk of developing mental health problems such as depression and anxiety. Another approach is to use real-life examples and personal stories to illustrate the impact of violence against women. This could involve sharing stories of women who have experienced abuse and the devastating effects it has had on their lives, as well as highlighting the importance of support and resources for survivors. In addition to presenting a strong argument, it is important to communicate your position clearly and persuasively. This means speaking confidently and using language that is accessible and easy to understand. It is also important to actively engage with your audience, whether that be judges or fellow debaters, by acknowledging their perspectives and responding to their points in a respectful and thoughtful way. Ultimately, winning a debate competition on violence against women is about more than just the competition itself. It is about using your voice to make a difference, to raise awareness of a critical issue, and to advocate for change. It is an opportunity to challenge harmful attitudes and behaviors, and to promote respect, equality, and dignity for all.
